Data Analysis Results

The following visualizations provide key insights into the space debris environment and the dynamics of space objects.

Altitude Distribution of Debris

This histogram illustrates the distribution of space debris objects across different orbital altitudes, revealing the concentration of debris in specific altitude ranges, particularly in Low Earth Orbit (LEO).

Altitude Distribution by RCS Size and Object Type

This box plot examines the relationship between orbital altitude, Reaction Control System (RCS) size, and object type (operational or non-operational) at various orbital altitudes.

Cumulative Debris by Country

This bar chart depicts the cumulative number of debris objects originating from different countries, highlighting the significant contribution of certain nations to the space debris population and underscoring the global responsibility for mitigating the issue.

Cumulative Object Count by Launch Year

This line graph illustrates the cumulative number of space objects launched each year, showcasing the exponential growth of the space debris population over time. This trend emphasizes the urgent need for sustainable space practices and debris mitigation strategies.

Period Distribution of Payloads

This histogram represents the distribution of orbital periods for various types of space payloads, providing insights into the operational characteristics and mission objectives of different space assets.
